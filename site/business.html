Business

The Company

Kellogg Company, founded in 1906 and incorporated in Delaware in 1922, and its subsidiaries are engaged in the manufacture and marketing of snacks and convenience foods. Unless otherwise specified or indicated by the context, "Kellogg," the "Company," "we," "us" and "our" refer to Kellogg Company its divisions and subsidiaries.

Principal Products

Our principal products are snacks, such as crackers, savory snacks, toaster pastries, cereal bars, granola bars and bites; and convenience food, such as, ready-to-eat cereals, frozen waffles, veggie foods and noodles. These products were, as of February 21, 2023, manufactured by us in 21 countries and marketed in more than 180 countries. They are sold to retailers thorugh direct sales forces for resale to consumers. We use broker and distributor arrangements for certain products and channels, as well as in certain geographies.

Our snacks brands are marketed under brands such as Kellogg's, Cheez-It, Pringles, Austin, Parati, and RXBAR. Our cereals and cereal bars are generally marketed under the Kellogg's name, with some under the Kashi and Bear Naked Brands. Our Frozen food are marketed under the Eggo and Morningstar Farms brands.

Demand for our products generally level throughout the year, although some of our convenience foods have a bias for stronger demand in the second half of the year due to events and holidays.

Customers

Our largest customer, Wal-Mart Stores, Inc. and its affiliates, accounted for approximately 18% of consolidated net sales during 2022, comprised principally of sales within the United States. No other customer accounted for greater than 10% of net sales in 2022.

During 2022, out top five customers, collectively, including Wal-Mart, accounted for approximately 32% of our consolidated net sanes and aproximately 53% of U.S. net sales. There has been significant worldwide consolidation in the forcery industry and we believe that this trend is likely to continue. Although the loss of any large customer for an extended length of time could negatively impact our sales and profits, we do not anticipate that this will occur to a significant extent due to the customer demand for our products and our relationships with out customers. Our products have been generally sold through our own sales forces and through broker and distributor arrangements, and have been generally resold to consumers in retail stores, restaurants, and other food service establishments.

Competition

Our products compete with advertised and branded products of a similar nature as well as unadvertised and private label products, which are typically distributed at lower prices, and generally with other food products. Principal methods and factors of competition include new product introductions, product quality, taste, convenience, nutrition value, price, advertising and promotion.
